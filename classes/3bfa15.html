About the course: In this course we will study the integral calculus, including definite and indefinite integrals, techniques of integration, the fundamental theorem of calculus, and applications in mathematics and physics.

Textbook: It is recommended that you have a calculus textbook to use for reference throughout the course. The official text for the class is Stewart's Single Variable Calculus (Seventh Edition). The version available in the bookstore is the custom "California edition." If you are thinking about using a different textbook, you may find this comparison to be helpful.

Enrollment: Questions about enrollment and the waitlist should be directed to Math Undergraduate Advising at ugrad@math.ucsb.edu. Priority on the waitlist will be given to those attending ("crashing") class and discussion section.

Discussion sections: Enrollment and attendance in one of the Monday discussion section is mandatory.

Homework: Homework will be assigned weekly, to be completed on the online service WebWork. Homework sets may be accessed through the GauchoSpace site for the course. They will generally be due at 11:30pm on Wednesday nights, subject to announced exceptions. The system will accept homework until 11:59pm, after which no late homework will be accepted. Quizzes: Weekly quizzes will be given in discussion section. Your lowest two quiz grades will be dropped.

Study suggestions: The most important thing for doing well in this class is keeping up with the homework. Make sure you understand how to do the homework problems without having the textbook open for reference (of course it's okay to do problems with the book open, but to make sure you actually "get it" you should make sure you can do some with the book closed). Many exam and quiz questions will look suspiciously like homework questions. Don't be afraid to do extra exercises from the textbook. Read examples from the textbook. Do a little bit every day instead of cramming homework and reading into one night a week. If there's something you're not clear on, ask about it in discussion section or office hours as soon as possible.

Helpful resources: You are encouraged to drop by either my office hours or your TA's office hours (or both!). Additionally, the Math Lab (South Hall 1607) is staffed by graduate students M-F, 12pm-5pm, and they are happy to help with any of the material from the course. You can also visit the Online Math Lab for online resources.

Grading: Your grade will be calculated as follows:

  • 10% weekly homework (lowest homework dropped)
  • 15% weekly quizzes (lowest two quizzes dropped)

  • 35% final exam
Curves for quiz and exam grades will be announced during the quarter. There is no preset number of A's, B's, etc., so you are not competing against your fellow students.
